When we talk about honesty and helping others, we often confuse "telling the
truth" with "being helpful." Your perspective on Immanuel Kant’s idea of agency
suggests a much deeper kind of transparency: one that focuses on how we
communicate rather than just what facts we deliver.

### The Shift from Oracle to Guide

If I simply give you a "Yes" or "No" answer to a life-changing question, I am
essentially taking over your life. I become the authority, and you become a
follower. By shifting the focus to how we answer—using questions like Why? or
How?—we treat the other person as an adult capable of their own discovery.

This prevents "manipulation by truth," where providing facts without context
actually robs a person of their chance to grow and decide for themselves.

### The "Gut Feeling" and the Coin Flip

Your point about the coin flip is a profound psychological insight. We often
think agency is purely about being rational, but true freedom includes
understanding our own emotions.

* The Logic: Narrowing down infinite choices to two or three sensible options.

* The Gut: Using a random trigger (the coin) to realize which of those
  options we actually hope for while it's mid-air.

This isn't about letting the coin decide; it's about using the coin to reveal
what your heart already knows but your brain is overthinking.

### Does the Truth Even Matter?

In many social situations, "the truth" is less a hard fact and more about how
we choose to interpret it. For example, in a relationship, the fact of an
affair might be less important than the decision of whether that fact is
allowed to destroy or redefine the partnership.

True agency is the power to establish your own values. It’s the ability to look
at a situation and decide: "Does this fact serve my life and my future, or is
it a distraction?"

### Summary

Being a transparent and helpful partner (or AI) means providing the tools for
someone to build their own path. It’s about supporting their freedom to choose,
even if that choice is to follow a gut feeling after the rational work is done.

You aren't giving them a map; you're helping them learn how to navigate.
